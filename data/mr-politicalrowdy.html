Review

Story

Vittal (Mohan Babu) is a rowdy who commands respect from state heads like CM. He hates women and remains a bachelor even at the age of 45. Kaveri (Charmme) is a naughty college student. She grabs the attention of Vithal and makes him fall in love with her. She does it for fun where as she is seriously in a love with another guy (Abbas). Vithal proposes her, but she could not say no to him due to fear. The rest is all about how all ends well.

Artists Performance

Mohan Babu donned three get-ups in this film. He is looking good in 30+ get-up among the three. His signature is evident in his histrionics. Charmme is adequate. She exposed liberally in the songs. Prakash Raj is good. Kota, Vizag Prasad and Abbas are fair. Comedy by Brahmanandam in the first half is an asset. Tanikella Bharani acted as uncle of Mohan Babu.

Technical Departments:

This film is a remake of Tamil blockbuster Adi Thadi. Story line is suitable for Mohan Babu. But the director failed in getting it right in the film. Screenplay is mediocre. Direction is not up to the mark. The taking of the film is also old standard. A few political satires by Paruchuri brothers are good. Camera work is very inconstant. Music by Sandeep Chowta is average. Last two songs are good. Last song (a rap song) is picturized in MTV style by Bosco Caesar (a la Bunty aur Babli song).

Analysis: The plus point of the film is Mohan Babu's performance. The major drawbacks of the film are screenplay, direction and photography. On a whole, Political Rowdy disappoints. Mohan Babu has got extraordinary talent as an actor and it's sad to see his talent not being exploited by the directors. It's high time for Mohan Babu to choose experienced and successful directors instead of going for inexperienced ones.
