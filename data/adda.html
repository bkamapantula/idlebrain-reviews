Review

Story

Abhi (Sushanth) is a breakup consultant who also is a wedding planner for the couple of who do register marriage. Priya (Shanvi) is the daughter of a big man. She approaches Abhi to breakup the love of her elder sister (Suhasini) who is in love with a software engineer (Srinivas Avasarala). The rest of the story is all about the reason why he choose register office as his Adda and what happens between Abhi and Priya.

Artists Performance

Actors: Sushanth’s new look is good. He is confident. His dialogue delivery is free-flowing. He has danced with good energy levels (especially title song). He has arrived as an actor with this movie. Shanvi suited the role of a rich girl who inevitably falls in love. Dev Gill starts off as a ruthless villain and ends up being a relatively soft guy. Jaya Prakash cameo as village head is hilarious. Raghu Babu entertains as sidekick of villain. The comedians gang of hero (Dhanraj, Thagubothu Ramesh and Jayam Venu) are good. Josh Ravi’s gay comedy is an extension of what he has done in Gunde Jaari Gallanthayyinde. Srinivas Avasarala and Suhasini are natural.

Technical departments

Story - screenplay - direction: It’s a kind of romantic comedy set up in a different backdrop. Hero breaking up couples gives a new angle to the story. Debutant director G Karthik Reddy does a decent job in selecting a commercial story with a different premise. He is good with entertainment too. Screenplay of the film is nice in the first half and should have been impact-making one in the second half (towards climax).

Other departments: Songs scored by Anup Rubens are very good and peppy. His background music is also very good. Anup Rubens knows the pulse of music lovers and all he need is a star film to catapult him into the next league. Dialogues written by Karthik Reddy are good. Cinematography by Arun Kumar is neat. Fights composed by Kanal Kannan are interesting. Editing by Gowtam Raju is okay. Producers Chintalapudi Srinivasa Rao and A Naga Susheela made sure that production values of the film are uncompromising.

Analysis
First half of the film is decent. The latter part of second half should have been handled better. The comedy and entertainment in the film is good. Plus points of the film are Sushanth, entertainment and music. On the flip side, scenes flow towards climax should have been better. On a whole, Adda film makes an okay watch for it’s entertainment and music.
