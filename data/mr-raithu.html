Raithu Rajyam - A Review
R Narayana Murthy
presents
Raithu Rajyam
Jeevi Rating: 2.5/5
Genre
Revolutionary
Jeevi Rating
2.5/5 (Two and half on a scale of five)
Cast
R Narayana Murthy, Sarat Babu, Gokina Rama Rao, Jeeva and Kalpana
Credits
Photography: Chiranjeevi
Music: Sheik Imam
Dialogues: LB Sri Ram
Story - Screenplay - Production & Direction: R Narayana Murthy
Theatrical Release Date
10th December 1999
Review

This film is the clash between the CM and the farmers. CM of the state is shown as a person who exploits the farmers. Ramudu(R Narayana Murthy) is a farmer who has given away all his money to his siblings. Ramudu married a girl whose father suicides after drinking poison.

Sivaji is a relative of Ramudu who is a government employee who father was murdered in a feud. Sivaji and Ramudu unites to teach lesson to the CM. They start a party called 'Raithu Rajyam'. 'Raithu Rajyam' wins with a marginal majority. Sivaji wants to become the CM. But he cannot become CM unless he gets support from the ex-CM. And Sivaji manages to get it.

Ramudu desserts Sivaji joins the Naxalites movement. Meantime Sivaji resigns to his post and fresh elections are announced. During elections Sivaji gets murdered by the people of ex-CM. Ramudu fights for election free society.

This films end with ramudu taking revenge on all evil forces.

Analysis
This film is a typical Narayana Murthy film. R Narayana Murthy tried his best to drive a message across. Weakling of this film is the story. The director seems to have gone out of the scope of his story to criticize the CM character in the film. Dialogues by LB Sri Ram are powerful. Music is neat. All the actors did their bit well. This film is made for the farmers. We have to wait and see if this message oriented film reaches successfully to the masses.
