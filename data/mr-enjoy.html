Movie review - Enjoy
Enjoy
Credits

Jeevi rating: 1/5
Punchline: Pathetic film
Genre: Love
Can kids watch this film? Yes (no vulgarity and no violence)
Banner: Shiva Sai Productions
Cast: Ajay, Chitra, Badarinath, Jaya Prakash Reddy, Surya, Jackie, Brahmanandam, MS Narayana, Jenny, Ali, Gundu Hanumantha Rao, Goutham Raju, Kantha Rao, Ananth, Chitti Babu, Vallabhaneni Janardhan, Kavita, Ramyasri.
Story - screenplay: Shiva Sai Productions
Dialogues: Marudhuri Raja
Music: Chakri
Editing: Menaga Sreenu
Cinematography: K Prasad
Lyrics: Bhaskarabhatla Ravi Kumar, Kandikonda and Bandaru Danayya
Producers: G Sadasiva Reddy & B Srinivas Reddy
Direction: TN Raju
Release date: 5th March 2004
Review

Story

Ajay (Ajay) is the brother of Jackie (Jackie), who is a henchman working for local goon (Jayaprakash Reddy). Chitra (Chitra) is the sister of another don (Surya). Ajay and Chitra fall in love. Chitra's brother wants Ajay to become a millionaire before marrying Chitra. Jackie cheats his boss and run away with 2 crores so that he could give that money to Ajay. Jackie hides that money in a crematory ground. The don kills Jackie. Jackie tells about the treasure to a eunuch (Ali) before dying. That eunuch takes the money and keeps searching for Ajay. Meanwhile, Ajay and Chitra elope. The rest of the film is about how Ajay convinces the brother of Chitra.

Artists Performance

Ajay is the younger brother of comedian Ali. He acted with his real name 'Khayyum' in a few films earlier. As a hero this is his debut film. He is decent as an actor. His dances are good. But, his body language does not suit the stature of hero. Heroine Chitra is very artificial in her expressions. Jaya Prakash Reddy is fair. Surya is impressive. The comedy track by Ali, Brahmanandam and MS Narayana is very stale. Kavita is compassionate as mother.

Technical Departments:

Screenplay of the film is mediocre and direction is pathetic. Narration is uninteresting. Music by Chakri is above average. You do expect some good punches in Marudhuri Raja's work. But dialogues in this film are bad. Cinematography is average.

Analysis: The title 'Enjoy' has no relevance to the story of this film. First half and second half are equally bad. This film does not have any thing to offer. 'Enjoy' would test your patience. Stay away!
