Credits

Jeevi rating: 2/5
Punchline: Old cop-story with a new hero
Genre: Action
Can kids watch this film? Yes (no vulgarity and no violence)
Banner: AA Arts
Cast: Brahmaji, Sindhu Menon, Bhanu Chandar, Ananda Raj, Jenny, Duvvasi Mohan, Kondavalasa Lakshmana Rao
Music: Vandemataram Srinivas
Stunts: Ram Lakshman
Producer: Mahendra
Screenplay - Direction: KS Nageswara Rao
Release date: 10th October 2003
Review

Story

Azad Chandra Sekhar (Brahmaji) is a honest and powerful cop. Pandey (Ananda Raj) is a mafia guy. Sivani (Sindhu) is a lawyer. Azad is on the prowl to attack Pandey's atrocities by clipping his wings. In the process, Pandey rapes Sivani. Azad marries Sivani in order to give a respectable social status to her. The rest of the story is all about how Azad puts an end to the life of Pandey.

Artists Performance

Brahmaji: For the first time, Brahmaji played the role of solo hero in his career. Incidentally Brahmaji played the cop roles in his last two films - Johnny and Nijam. Brahmaji is impressive and his dialogue delivery also suited the characterization. His catch word in this film is 'tolu teesta'.

Others: Sindhu Menon is OK. Ananda Raj is adequate. Bhanu Chandar did hero's brother. He is good except for the jarring dialogue delivery. Kondavalasa Lakshmana Rao played the role of 'Johnny' (a head constable) in this film. Narasimha Raju - as the father of heroine - is fair.

Technical Departments:

Story: It's a routine story of a police inspector we see from the days of 'Ankusam'. Its very predictable.

Screenplay - direction: The taking of this film is meant to entertain the front-benchers. The direction of the film is average. The narration of the film is so quick that it took 1 hour 45 minutes narrate it. Screenplay is mediocre.

Other departments: Background score by Vandemataram is so so. He recycled old scores as background music in this film. Dialogues are OK at times and average most of the times. Editing is not good. There quite a few jumps in the editing of this film. Fights by Ram Lakshman are adequate. Production of the film is done on low budget.

Analysis: The film is an old cop story made with a new hero. 'Inspector' offers no excitement. Brahmaji might shine in future as a solo hero, if he chooses good scripts. The best part of the film is its duration - 1:45 hrs. It's a firm 'no' for A centers. It might do well in B and C centers, because of its mass quotient.
